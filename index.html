Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Bright Future Academy

What is the admission process for Bright Future Academy?

Our admission process includes completing an online application form, submitting required documents (birth certificate, previous school records, passport photographs), taking an entrance examination, and attending an interview. Applications are open year-round, but we recommend applying early for the next academic session.

What are the school fees and payment options?

School fees vary by level: Primary (₦450,000/year), Junior Secondary (₦550,000/year), and Senior Secondary (₦650,000/year). We offer flexible payment plans including termly installments. Fees cover tuition, learning materials, sports, and extracurricular activities. Visit our Fees page for detailed breakdown.

What extracurricular activities are available?

We offer a wide range of activities including sports (football, basketball, athletics, swimming), arts (music, drama, visual arts), clubs (debate, science, robotics, chess), and leadership programs. All students are encouraged to participate in at least one extracurricular activity.

What is the student-to-teacher ratio?

We maintain a low student-teacher ratio of 20:1 to ensure personalized attention for each student. Our classes are designed to facilitate interactive learning and allow teachers to address individual student needs effectively.

Does the school provide transportation?

Yes, we offer safe and reliable school bus services covering major areas in Lagos including Victoria Island, Lekki, Ikoyi, Surulere, and Ikeja. Transportation fees are separate from tuition and vary based on distance. Our buses are equipped with GPS tracking and supervised by trained staff.

What curriculum does Bright Future Academy follow?

We follow the Nigerian National Curriculum with enhancements in STEM education, critical thinking, and character development. Our Senior Secondary students are prepared for WAEC, NECO, and JAMB examinations with a 98% pass rate.
